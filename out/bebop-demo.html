History of Bebop

Bebop emerged in the 1940s as a revolutionary jazz style that emphasized complex harmonies, virtuosic instrumental technique, and small ensemble playing. Pioneers like Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, and Thelonious Monk developed this style as a reaction to the simpler swing era.

Key characteristics include fast tempos (160-300+ BPM), eighth-note runs, chromatic enclosures (approaching chord tones from a half-step above or below), syncopated rhythms, and harmonic sophistication. This line demonstrates typical bebop phrasing with chromatic passing tones and rhythmic complexity.
