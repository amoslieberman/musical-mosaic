The ii-V-I Progression

The ii-V-I is the foundation of jazz improvisation. It appears in countless jazz standards and is essential for any jazz musician to master.

In Bb Major:
  • • Cm7 (ii) - minor 7th chord
  • • F7 (V) - dominant 7th chord
  • • Bbmaj7 (I) - major 7th chord (resolution)

This progression creates harmonic motion and tension/release that's perfect for improvisation. The V chord (F7) pulls strongly toward the I chord (Bbmaj7).

Syncopation in Bebop

  • Strategic Rests: Silences create rhythmic interest and prevent the line from sounding predictable
  • Off-Beat Emphasis: Notes starting on the "and" of beats create swing feel
  • Chromatic Enclosures: Approaching target notes from a half-step above or below (e.g., B-C to resolve to C)
  • Rhythmic Variation: Mixing eighth notes, sixteenth notes, and quarter notes prevents monotony
  • Phrase Breathing: Each chord gets its own melodic idea with clear resolution points
