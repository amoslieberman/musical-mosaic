About Chord Symbols

Chord symbols appear above each measure and indicate the harmonic content. They're essential for:

  • • Understanding harmonic progressions
  • • Improvisation guidance for soloists
  • • Comping (accompaniment) patterns for rhythm section
  • • Reharmonization opportunities

Chord Types Used

  • maj7 - Major 7th (major chord + major 7th)
  • m7 - Minor 7th (minor chord + minor 7th)
  • 7 - Dominant 7th (major chord + minor 7th)

The Progression

This is a classic ii-V-I jazz progression with variations, commonly found in jazz standards:

| Gmaj7 | Am7 | D7 | Gmaj7 | Bm7 | E7 | Am7 | D7 |

The D7 → Gmaj7 is the classic V-I (dominant to tonic) resolution. The Bm7 → E7 is a secondary dominant taking you to Am7.
