  • Structure: Major 6th chord + Diminished 7th chord on the major 2nd

Why Barry Harris Used This Scale

Barry Harris developed this scale as a fundamental concept in modern jazz harmony. The combination of a major 6th chord with a diminished 7th chord built on the major 2nd creates a unique symmetry that works beautifully over major chords with extensions (maj7, maj9, maj13).

This scale is particularly useful because the two 4-note chords (major 6th and diminished 7th) create interesting harmonic and melodic possibilities, making it incredibly versatile for improvisation and composition in a jazz context.
